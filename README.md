## ðŸ˜Ž Team working as proud geeks - Keep open mind

As most of us being proud of diving into our problems, and present our solutions.  Over time,
we learn a few tools to align colleagues/fellow hackers with our thoughts. Here are a few:


### Focus on the right problem to work on

> If I had an hour to solve a problem I'd spend 55 minutes thinking about the problem and 5 minutes thinking about solutions
â€• Albert Einstein

It is a surprisingly difficult skill to learn how to work on the right problem.  Here are a few tips:

* [ðŸ“œ focus on problem and learn from customers](https://blog.leanstack.com/love-the-problem-not-your-solution-65cfbfb1916b)

* Whiteboarding and Canvasing is a
great way to open our mind.  More at [ðŸ“œ Introducing the Deep Learning Canvas - a variation on Startup Canvas](https://medium.com/intuitionmachine/introducing-the-deep-learning-canvas-a2e80a998f11) - You can print this out or grab a whiteboard and draw this out.

* Data [ðŸ“œ Design sprint](https://designsprintkit.withgoogle.com/) - Keeping open minded.  We also enjoy a minimal version of this [ðŸ“œ The 25-Minute Design Sprint](https://www.uxpin.com/studio/blog/the-25-minute-design-sprint/) which we find it helpful to adjust and adapt.


### Communicating the results

> I'm not a great programmer; I'm just a good programmer with great habits - Kent Beck

Writing a readable notebook and explaining the result is a great
habit.  [ðŸ“œ Clean code in Jupyter notebooks](https://www.slideshare.net/katenerush/clean-code-in-jupyter-notebooks) in our go to guide
